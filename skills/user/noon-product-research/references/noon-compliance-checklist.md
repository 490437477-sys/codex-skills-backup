# noon Compliance & Regulatory Reference

Use this checklist during Step 7 of the noon-product-research workflow. Mark each item Must-Have / Should-Have / N/A. Must-Have items are launch blockers; Should-Have can be addressed after first sale.

## 1. Saudi Arabia (noon.sa)

### SASO (Saudi Standards, Metrology and Quality Organization)
- **SASO IECEE**: Required for most mains-powered electronics (laptops, phones, chargers, kitchen appliances, hair dryers). Lead time 4-8 weeks.
- **Energy efficiency labels**: Required for ACs, refrigerators, washing machines, lighting.
- **Toys safety (EN 71 / ASTM F963)**: Required for toys; lead time 4-6 weeks.
- **Children's products**: Additional conformity for items used by under-14s.

### SFDA (Saudi Food and Drug Authority)
- Required for: food, supplements, cosmetics, personal care, medical devices, pharmaceuticals.
- Cosmetics: SFDA cosmetic product notification, lead time 6-12 weeks for new brands.
- Food: SFDA import + product registration; perishables need cold-chain declaration.

### CITC (Communications, Space and Technology Commission)
- Required for: phones, tablets, smart watches, radio devices (Wi-Fi/Bluetooth).
- Type approval + IMEI registration; lead time 3-6 weeks.

### Customs & Import
- SABER platform: SABER conformity certificate required before shipment clears Saudi customs.
- HS code determines duty rate (0-25%); verify per SKU before committing inventory.
- Arabic label mandatory on most consumer goods at point of import.

### Restricted / Prohibited
- Alcohol, pork, gambling products, weapons, adult content, single-use plastics (partial), tobacco (special licensing).
- Religious items (non-functional) require review; cross-border sellers face additional scrutiny.

## 2. UAE (noon.ae)

### MOIAT (Ministry of Industry and Advanced Technology, formerly ESMA)
- **ECAS / EQM**: Required for regulated product categories (electronics, toys, cosmetics, automotive parts).
- Lead time 3-6 weeks.

### Telecommunications and Digital Government Regulatory Authority (TDRA)
- Required for: phones, tablets, wireless devices.
- Type approval certificate; lead time 2-4 weeks.

### Food & Cosmetics
- Dubai Municipality registration for food + cosmetics.
- Abu Dhabi Department of Health for medical devices.
- Lead time varies 4-12 weeks.

### Restricted
- Alcohol (only via licensed retailers), pork, gambling, weapons.
- Cross-border sellers without a UAE trade license face additional restrictions on some categories.

## 3. Egypt (noon.com)

### GOEIC (General Organization for Export and Import Control)
- **Importer registration**: Required for many product categories; factories must be GOEIC-registered in some cases (impacting Chinese cross-border sellers).
- Lead time 8-16 weeks for first registration.

### NFSA (National Food Safety Authority)
- Required for all food products; lead time 6-12 weeks.

### EDA (Egyptian Drug Authority)
- Required for cosmetics, medical devices, pharmaceuticals.

### Currency & Customs
- Importers must repatriate export revenue; some categories have minimum local content.
- Verify per-HS-code duty rates; cosmetics and electronics face the highest scrutiny.

## 4. GCC-Wide Rules

### GSO (GCC Standardization Organization)
- Regional standards that often underlie KSA / UAE national rules. Conformity to GSO standards is generally accepted across GCC.

### Arabic Labeling
- Arabic label mandatory on most consumer goods in KSA and UAE.
- Minimum content: product name, weight/volume, ingredients (food/cosmetics), manufacturer + importer, country of origin, batch/lot number, expiry (if applicable).

### Halal
- Not a regulatory requirement, but a market expectation for food, cosmetics, and personal care.
- Halal certificate from an accredited body (JAKIM, MUI, GAC) carries weight.

## 5. noon-Specific Seller Obligations

- **Seller agreement**: confirm category approval before sourcing inventory.
- **Arabic listing copy**: required for FBN-eligible SKUs in many categories.
- **FBN labeling**: 2D barcode + noon SKU label required; incorrect labeling triggers unplanned fees.
- **Packaging**: must withstand Gulf heat (recommend insulated / reinforced for cosmetics, food, electronics).
- **Returns**: FBN-fulfilled returns go back to noon warehouse; cross-border returns are the seller's responsibility unless otherwise negotiated.

## 6. Pre-Launch Compliance Checklist

Use this as a final gate before sourcing:

- [ ] SASO IECEE (KSA) or ECAS (UAE) certificate in hand for electronics
- [ ] SFDA / NFSA / EDA notification in hand for regulated FMCG
- [ ] CITC / TDRA type approval in hand for wireless devices
- [ ] SABER certificate ready for KSA customs clearance
- [ ] Arabic label artwork approved
- [ ] Halal certificate on file (food, cosmetics)
- [ ] Importer of record (IOR) appointed in destination country
- [ ] HS code + duty rate confirmed for the SKU
- [ ] noon category approval confirmed in Seller Central
- [ ] noon FBN SKU label + 2D barcode generation tested

If any Must-Have line is unchecked, hold the launch.
