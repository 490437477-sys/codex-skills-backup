# Mercado Pago Installments Strategy

Reference for structuring cuotas sin interés (interest-free installments) offers across Mercado Libre sites. Numbers are approximate; verify with current Mercado Pago rate card.

## 1. Why Installments Matter on Mercado Libre

- Average ticket (AOV) on Mercado Libre is significantly higher than peers precisely because of widespread installment use
- Listings with cuotas sin interés rank visibly better and convert 15–30% higher (varies by category)
- Buyers sort by installment count; showing 12 cuotas even on small tickets lifts visibility
- Installment offerings are a top-3 filter in search results, ahead of free shipping in many categories

## 2. Installment Lengths Available by Site

| MLM (Mexico) | 3, 6, 9, 12, 18, 24 | MSI (Meses Sin Intereses) is the standard marketing term |

## 3. Who Pays the Interest

Three operating modes a seller must understand:

- Seller absorbs — You list at full price; Mercado Pago charges interest to the buyer but you receive full ticket. Common in MSI promotions.
- Buyer pays — Buyer sees monthly payment with interest; you receive net of the financing fee.
- Promotional bank — A bank such as BBVA, Santander, Banco de Chile, Banco do Brasil subsidizes the interest during a promotional window. The listing shows cuotas sin interés because the bank pays the cost.

## 4. Decision Matrix by Price Tier

| Price tier | Recommended cuotas | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Under 500 MXN / 100 BRL | 3 cuotas | Below threshold for longer plans; MSI drains margin |
| 500–3,000 MXN / 100–500 BRL | 6 cuotas | Sweet spot for volume goods |
| 3,000–10,000 MXN / 500–1,500 BRL | 12 cuotas | Mid-ticket; absorbs interest if margin allows |
| Over 10,000 MXN / 1,500 BRL | 12 or 18 cuotas | Big-ticket; consider 18/24 for white goods and electronics |

## 5. Margin Math

Let P = price, C = cost of goods, F = Mercado commission (~13–17%), M = payment processing (~3–5% on top).

```
Effective margin = (P − C − P*F − P*M) / P
```

If seller absorbs interest, Mercado Pago typically charges the seller 5–25% of P depending on term length and risk band. Always recompute with the actual rate card.

- 3 cuotas seller cost ≈ 3–8%
- 6 cuotas seller cost ≈ 6–14%
- 12 cuotas seller cost ≈ 12–22%
- 18+ cuotas seller cost ≈ 18–30%

## 6. Category Fit

- Strong fit — Electronics, white goods, furniture, tires, mattresses, smartphones
- Moderate fit — Fashion, sports, beauty bundles
- Weak fit — Low AOV consumables; commissions plus financing erase margin
- Avoid — Refurbished / used goods under 1,000 MXN; bank MSI restrictions apply

## 7. Hot Sale and Buen Fin Strategy

- Hot Sale Mexico — Early May, 4-day event. Mercado negotiates bank MSI extensions up to 24 cuotas in select categories. Plan pricing so the longest cuotas still produces positive contribution margin.
- Buen Fin Mexico — Mid November, 4-day event, mirrors Black Friday. Same MSI playbook but earlier holiday inventory deadline.
- Black Friday Brazil — late November; parcelamento sem juros 10–12 is expected by buyers
- Cyber Monday and Día de las Madres — secondary peaks; 6–12 cuotas performs well

## 8. Listing Wiring Checklist

- Set cuotas sin interés on every listing above the threshold
- Promote the longest cuota prominently in the listing image; buyers self-select
- Coordinate with bank MSI calendars; only advertise cuotas sin interés when the promo is actually active
- Test 6 vs 12 on the same SKU with ads; conversion lift usually justifies the financing cost above ~2,000 MXN

## 9. Common Pitfalls

- Advertising cuotas sin interés that are not actually active — leads to claims and reputation damage
- Forgetting to update after a bank MSI window expires
- Setting cuotas that push effective margin negative on already discounted goods
- Mixing currencies incorrectly when using cross-border Full inventory

## 10. Bank MSI Calendar Highlights

- Mexico — BBVA, Santander, Banorte, HSBC, Banregio. Each runs MSI calendars aligned with Hot Sale (May) and Buen Fin (November). Confirm with bank portal weekly during campaign.
- Brazil — Banco do Brasil, Itaú, Bradesco, Caixa. Parcelamento sem juros very common on Black Friday.
- Argentina — Galicia, Macro, Santander Argentina. Cuotas sin interés driven by Ahora 12 / Ahora 18 government program.
- Chile — Banco de Chile, Santander, BCI. Cuotas sin interés during Cyber Day and Black Friday.
- Colombia — Bancolombia, Davivienda. Cuotas sin interés during Día sin IVA days.

## 11. Risk and Cash Flow

- Installments do not slow seller cash flow — Mercado settles seller in the same window regardless of buyer term
- Mercado pays seller the full ticket (when seller absorbs) minus commission and financing fee
- Cash-flow planning: set payouts weekly or bi-weekly; do not over-commit inventory to MSI campaigns without margin headroom

## 12. Quick Reference

- Mexico MSI term: Meses Sin Intereses
- Brazil: Parcelamento sem juros
- Spanish-speaking sites: Cuotas sin interés
- Always display the longest active cuota in the hero image
- Reconcile payout files weekly; financing cost is itemized in Mercado Pago settlement
